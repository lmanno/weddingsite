Please know that there are many other great hotels in San Francisco, Napa, Sonoma, and the surrounding towns, so feel free to reach out with any questions about the area!

What should we do for fun?

Things to do

Up in wine country, we recommend going for a bike ride or tasting some wine or beer. The Santa Rosa Cycling Club has a great list of longer rides on their website, or, if you're looking for a shorter ride, we recommend this this 13-mile loop that starts at the Sonoma Valley Cyclery (where you can rent bikes) and passes by five wineries.

The towns of Healdsburg, Napa, Sonoma, and St. Helena are also worth a quick visit, as are the hot springs and spas in Calistoga. As for food, we recommend The Girl and the Fig, the Fremont Diner, and La Salette in Sonoma, Farmstead in St. Helena, and Mustards Grill in Napa.

Sugarloaf and Annadel State Parks are great for fun hiking and mountain biking. There's also a lot to do in Sonoma Plaza in downtown Sonoma.

Down in the city, Golden Gate Park, Land's End, Twin Peaks and Mt. Sutro, and the Presidio are all great places for a walk. If you like bikes, renting some and riding over the Golden Gate Bridge to Marin is cool. You can get a margarita in Tiburon before catching the ferry back to the city. There's great food everywhere, but we particularly like Arizmendi Bakery, B*Star, Zazie, Nopalito, and most of the Mexican food in the Mission. Laura will tell you to go to La Taqueria, Luke to Taqueria Cancun. Just to be safe, you should probably try both.
